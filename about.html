Dr. Jane Smith, PhD

Public Health Researcher | Epidemiologist | Health Policy Analyst

About Me

I am a public health researcher with a PhD in Public Health specializing in epidemiology and health policy. My work focuses on understanding and addressing health disparities through evidence-based research and policy recommendations.

With over a decade of experience in both academic and applied public health settings, I've developed expertise in quantitative research methods, health systems analysis, and program evaluation. My research has been published in leading public health journals and has informed policy decisions at local and national levels.

Currently, I serve as a Research Fellow at the Center for Public Health Innovation, where I lead studies examining the social determinants of health and evaluate the effectiveness of community health interventions. I'm passionate about translating research findings into actionable strategies that improve population health outcomes.

Research Philosophy

I believe that rigorous, interdisciplinary research is essential for addressing complex public health challenges. My approach combines quantitative epidemiological methods with qualitative insights to develop comprehensive understandings of health issues.

I'm particularly interested in how structural factors—such as economic policies, urban planning decisions, and healthcare system design—create and perpetuate health inequalities. By identifying these root causes, we can develop more effective, sustainable solutions.
